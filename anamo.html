SECTION 02 / Terrain Intel

The Trail

MODERATE DIFFICULTY
Piton de la Riviere Noire
~8 kmReturn
3 to 4 hrsWalking time
828 mSummit
FreeEntry
Starts on B103 at Plaine Champagne. Rainforest canopy for most of the ascent, good shade, humid. Muddy sections and exposed roots throughout. Final stretch steepens before the summit opens to full panoramic views. Marked with painted rocks and yellow ribbons. No technical climbing. No guide required.
If anyone wants to turn back early, that is a valid call. The forest is the experience. The summit is the bonus. There is no wrong version of this hike.

SECTION 06 / Unit Protocols

A Strong Unit Covers Its Own

Why This Section Exists
A strong unit is not one where everyone operates the same way. It is one where each member knows what the others need and responds without being asked. Ryan, Roy, and Luna each have a different operating mode. None of those modes is a flaw. What follows is the field manual: what to look for, and exactly what to do. Read it once. Know it. Use it when it matters.
Luna
If Luna goes into shutdown
She will go quiet and slow down. She does not need fixing, entertaining, or checking on. Roy: say "take your time" and match her pace. That is it. Ryan: nothing required, keep moving at your own pace. Luna will come back online on her own. Do not make it a moment. The hike continues.
Roy
If Roy hits overload or starts to spiral
He will talk faster, jump between topics, or start catastrophising. Luna: give him one concrete fact. "We are on the trail. The app shows we are here. We keep going." One sentence. Do not match his energy, anchor it. Ryan: no action needed. Your calm already helps more than you know.
Ryan
If Ryan needs to stop or step back
He will slow or stop without explanation. That is the signal. Do not ask why. Do not offer solutions. Do not fill the silence. Find a flat spot nearby, stop, give him space. Wait. When he starts moving again, that is the all-clear. Luna and Roy: no words needed. Just wait.
